Graphs and Diagrams

Click on the image for a closer look!

A schematic of the experimental set up within Cave-C showing the detectors involved to measure the inverse kinematically complete QFS reactions of the Radio-Active-Ion beam produced by GSI.
A diagram of the active area in the Multi-Sampling-Ionisation-Chamber (MUSIC) detector
This is a scatter plot of the mean energy loss values from each fitted peak vs the peaks corresponding atomic number squared. This plot shows a linear dependency where energy loss is directly proportional to charge squared.
A 2D histogram of CAL energy loss data. This shows the application of calibrating the anodes with a gain match to the MAP data. This shows that each anode measures the same energy loss for a particle travelling through MUSIC.
This is an evolved plot of Isotope identification. It is a 2D histogram of HIT data which is where corresponding velocities are appliedis applied to the CAL data. It shows a velocity dependency on measured energy loss of the incoming and outgoing particles.
A 2D histogram showing the intensity of charges of particles vs their corresponding velocities. A calibrated velocity has now been applied and has now produced a horizontal trend of results, showing no increased velocity dependencies.
Charge vs velocity of particles using the velocity of measured particles by time-of-flight detectors 2 and 4. This has increased the resolution and the isotopes are shown much clearer and more recognizable.
This shows the final resolutions of energy loss measurements within the TWIM detector for isotopes of atomic charge Z=20. There is a better resolution when applying velocities measured with time-of-flight detectors 2 and 4 compared to with 3 and 4. The larger sigma separation cements it is of a higher resolution and has greater separation between charges.
